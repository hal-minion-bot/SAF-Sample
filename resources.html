Canadian Government Programs

Key support and funding programs available to seniors and caregivers

Ontario Health at Home (Formerly CCAC)

Funds and coordinates home and community care services in Ontario. Covers nursing, personal care, physiotherapy, and support services.

Eligibility: Ontario residents with health care needs

Assistive Devices Program (ADP)

Provincial program that partially funds assistive devices like walkers, wheelchairs, hearing aids, and communication devices.

Eligibility: Ontario residents with permanent disability

Ontario Drug Benefit (ODB)

Covers eligible prescription medications for Ontario seniors age 65+, with small co-payments based on income.

Eligibility: Ontario residents age 65+ with valid health card

Canada Caregiver Credit (Tax)

Federal tax credit for those who care for dependent seniors with physical or mental impairment.

Eligibility: Canadian resident providing unpaid caregiving

EI Compassionate Care Benefits

Temporary income support if you need to take time off work to care for a gravely ill family member.

Eligibility: Employed Canadian with 600 insurable hours in past year

Veterans Affairs Canada (VAC)

Veteran-specific programs including long-term care, home support, disability benefits, and income replacement.

Eligibility: Canadian Armed Forces or RCMP service members

Provincial Home Care Programs

How to access care services in your province

🔵 Ontario

Ontario Health at Home coordinates publicly-funded home and community care services.

Access: Ask your doctor for a referral or call your local Ontario Health at Home office

Learn More →

🟣 British Columbia

Community Health Services provide home support, nursing, therapy, and adult day programs.

Access: Contact your local health authority or your primary care provider

Learn More →

🟡 Alberta

Alberta Health Services provides home care through Continuing Care Services.

Access: Referral from your physician or direct contact with your zone's office

Learn More →

🟢 Saskatchewan

Saskatchewan Health Authority offers home care through Community Care Services.

Access: Through your family doctor or direct contact with SHA offices

Learn More →

🔴 Manitoba

Manitoba Health provides home care services through Community Health Programs.

Access: Referral from physician or self-referral through regional offices

Learn More →

⚪ Nova Scotia

Nova Scotia Health provides home support, nursing, therapy, and palliative care.

Access: Through your primary care provider or NS Health office
